Your portable architecture and urbanism guide when you travel

FINAL PROJECT FOR UOC POSTGRADUATE DIPLOMA @ UOC UNIVERSITY
“Diseño web: interfaces, usuarios y móviles”

Problem

From my personal experience as an architect, I have often missed an updated and digital architectural guide, a place where I could find useful suggestions about the architecture stuff of a certain area that I was visiting. The usual thing was buying a paper - outdated and heavy-to-carry - guide or "jumping" from the Google Maps points of interest and reviews to a random search on the browser, but that discontinuous and unsatisfactory process lead me to think that a dedicated app was needed.

Possible solution

The project's objective is to create the graphical interface and of a responsive web application, whose main functionality is to help the user find informations and suggestions about architectural elements in a certain area. Thanks to the descriptions and opinions of the users themselves, and therefore through an open database, it would be possible to have an up-to-date architectural guide and an interactive map, accessible to everyone and in any situation.

Design Thinking Process

  • Mostly desktop apps

Main functions / goals

  • Encourage competition in the market for the development of better products in this field
  • Create an extensive loyal community who collaborate on the database of the app

Statystical analysis

* The app is in its first stage of developement intended for the Italian public, that's why the analysis had considered this segment of the population. It is one of the future lines to make it available in other countries too. *

As a result of the analysis of the data provided by ISTAT (Italian National Institute of statystics), the following conclusions have been reached for the definition of potential users. The main users of the application will be between 25 and 44 years old, since they coincide with the main ICT users and are those who travel the most. Despite the fact that 15-24 year olds are also assiduous Internet users, their often greater economic difficulties until the end of the studies suggest they would be secondary users. Similarly, the over 44, although they have more economic resources and possibilities to travel, they will also be considered as secondary users, due to difficulties in the use of ICTs and portable devices. The data also pointed out the more frequent use of ICT by people with a second level education or university studies.

The information on households leads to the assumption that many of the people who would use the application could be a couple with a child or an individual family nucleus, since they tend to travel more. At a professional level, the greater probability is that the user is an entrepreneur, self-employed, manager or employee, because these are the categories that make the most investment in travel and recreational activities.

Finally, the application will be of course intended for the use of both men and women, since one of the main goals is to be inclusive and there are no data which proves any gap in this context.

Personas

The application is not intended to be specific to architectural or engineering professionals.The main users will be between 25 and 44 years old, men and women, as they coincide with the main ICT users and correspond to the age group that travels the most. Two personas have been created to help understand the needs of the average user.

Laura Artelli

  • woman
  • 25 years old
  • living alone
  • exposition curator
  • intermed. knlowledge of IT
  • intern in a museum
  • likes art, sports
  • kind, happy, friendly

Laura is from Rome, currently lives in Venice, where she recently graduated in Fine Arts as a curator of expositions. Laura shares a flat with some friends in the center of Venice and she is doing a three-month internship at the Venice Biennale. She is very fond of art and "cultural" tourism, going by car or train with her friends for a weekend to visit cities and places where they hold events. Laura really likes to organize the trip with her friends and decide together what to visit, however sometimes it is difficult to combine everyone's ideas.

Key points

  • Laura needs to stay up-to-date on nearby cultural events
  • Create shared tours with friends
  • Learn about the architectural attractions in the places she visits

Davide is an architect, originally from the outskirts of Milan and currently lives in Milan, with his wife Giulia and their 5-year-old daughter Alice. Davide is self-employed, he works in a coworking in the city center together with other architects and designers. Every morning he goes to work by bike, and in the afternoon he leaves at 5.30pm to go pick up Alice at her grandparents'. When he gets home he needs to continue working. Davide and his family travel quite often and he is in charge of studying the routes, especially the attractions and museums. He would like to have an application that could help him save time when organizing visits, also to get tickets online in advance at a better price

  • Davide needs to reduce trip planning time
  • Know the architectural attractions in the places he visits
  • Organize the routes

Quotes

“ I’m afraid I won’t have time to organize the trip properly, since it takes so much time. ” “ It happened already that I would miss some of the attractions of a city because I didn’t find informations about it on Google Maps. ”

Key learnings

  • The target is specialized and non specialized people. It shouldn’t be too technical.
  • It should be useful both for short or long trips.
  • Time is really important, it’s important to condens information and a map all in one place.
  • The possibility to plan visits and share the planning is a good asset.
